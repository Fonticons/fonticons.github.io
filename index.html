Stay, Unicode, Stay. Good Unicode.

@supercodepoet wrote this on 12 May 2015 Tweet

One of the best things about my job is getting to deliver a feature our awesome users have asked for. Today, I am happy to announce changes to how unicode values our handled in your kits!

Before when you added icons to a kit we waited until you published the kit to assign a unicode value to it. The problem was that when you removed an earlier icon all the unicode values could get shuffled when publishing the kit again.

Now when you add an icon to a kit we generate its unicode value right then. You have the ability to change this value on the "Tweak Kit" tab of your kit's settings. You can also see these values in the kit's documentation found under the "Publish Kit" tab.

With this change we hope make the transition from regular Font Awesome to using Fonticons much easier. We also hope that this will allow our users to do some new and amazing things in their CSS with their kits.

We try very hard to make Fonticons the best it can be. We're always listening to new suggestions, so keep'em coming!

Copy & Paste from Illustrator CC, Enterprise Services, and Free Accounts

@davegandy wrote this on 20 Apr 2015 Tweet

Copy & Paste from Illustrator CC!

Just minutes ago, we added the ability to copy from Illustrator CC and paste directly into a new custom icon on Fonticons!

All you have to do is:

  • Open up your vector file in Illustrator.
  • Select and copy the icon you'd like to add to Fonticons. The icon needs to be a single color vector with non-overlapping paths.
  • Switch over to Fonticons and click on "Your Custom Icons," then "Add Icon."
  • Name your icon, then paste into the "Paste from Illustrator CC" field.
  • Submit your icon, and you're good to go!

Black Tie!

@davegandy wrote this on 04 Mar 2015 Tweet

We're super excited to announce the addition of Black Tie to Fonticons! It is immediately available for folks on the Freelancer, Studio, and Agency plans!

Black Tie?

Black Tie is the first commercial icon set I've created, and I'm really proud of it. The big idea behind Black Tie is that icons should come in different visual weights, just as different font weights are an important part of traditional typography.

Stylistically, my goal with Black Tie was to create a clean, minimalist set of icons that will feel right at home next to modern sans-serif fonts like Helvetica and Proxima Nova. You can check out the Kickstarter page to see the story.
